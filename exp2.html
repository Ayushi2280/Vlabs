Aim:

To calculate various parameters when Zener Diode is functioning as a Voltage Regulator.


Objective:

Our project’s primary objective is to present the functionalities of a Zener Diode of how it can act as a voltage regulator by controlling the amount of flow of voltage across the whole circuit.


Theory

A normal semiconductor generally blocks the flow of current in the reverse direction but it will suffer from breakdown or damage if the reverse voltage is high in quantity. However, Zener Diodes are specially designed to have a low and specified Reverse Breakdown Voltage which takes advantage of reverse voltage applied to it. When the reverse voltage reaches a predetermined value the Zener diode starts to conduct electricity in reverse direction.

Now Zener Diodes can be used to produce a stabilized voltage under varying load conditions. By passing a small current through the diode via a current limiting resistor (Rs), the Zener Diode will conduct sufficient current to maintain a voltage drop out.

By connecting a Zener Diode in the circuit as shown below, a more stable output voltage can be obtained.



Here resistor Rs is connected in series with Zener diode to limit the flow of current in the circuit through the diode. Output is taken from RL across the Zener diode.

When there is no load connected the load current will be zero and everything will pass through the Zener diode only. A smaller Rs will cause higher diode current with resistance of load RL. So appropriate values must be considered so that Zener’ s maximum power rating (the maximum power till which a Zener diode can work perfectly) is exceeded.

Now as discussed earlier since a Zener diode is always operated in reverse biased condition as the fact that it can conduct while being in the reverse breakdown stage, a simple voltage regulator can a made using the Zener diode to maintain a constant output.

The Zener voltage regulator consists of a current limiting resistor RS connected in series with the input voltage (generally a dc battery) with the Zener diode connected in parallel with the load RL in this reverse biased condition.
